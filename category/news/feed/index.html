Comilla:  A child died 12 days after being admitted to Holy Care Hospital in Tomsombridge area of ​​the city. The family claims that the child died because of the doctor’s mistake.

They say that their son was suffering from heart problem. But for the last 12 days doctors were treating his cold without proper treatment.

Sunday (November 12) at 5 pm. The child named Hossain died.  

Goliara South Union of Sadar South Upazila of Comilla said. Hossein son of Soleimani. He was 2 months and 14 days old.

Soleman, the father of the child, said that on October 28, I brought my son to Holy Care Hospital with a cold. Then Dr. On the advice of Abdul Qayyum, he was admitted to the hospital. My son was admitted to the intensive care unit (ICU) on 10 November. Doctors did not do any echo or heart test before this. They suddenly told us on October 10 that three quarters of my child’s heart was damaged.  

In response to the question why wrong treatment or blaming the doctor, he said, if the doctor had told us about my child’s heart problem on the first day, we would have taken him to Dhaka or taken him elsewhere for treatment. But they didn’t tell us anything. No tests were done to diagnose my child’s heart problem. We will take legal action in this matter.  

Child’s mother Seema Akter said, I used to sell blood for my child. We did what the doctor said. But the doctor did not treat our child on time. We knew about cold and cough, and heard about heart problem two days ago.  

Regarding the complaint of the dead child’s parents, the accused doctor Qayyum could not be found.

Meanwhile, after hearing the news of the child’s death, when he went to the hospital on Sunday to collect news, there was a scuffle with a TV channel journalist and the channel’s camera was allegedly taken away by the director of the hospital. Against Morshedul Alam.  

The television channel’s cameraperson was injured in the incident. Colleagues took him to Comilla Sadar Hospital.  

President of Comilla Press Club Lutfar Rahman strongly condemned the incident of misbehaving with the journalist and taking away the camera in the hospital.  

Hospital director Dr. Morshedul Alam admitted to taking away the camera and said, I asked the journalist to meet me downstairs. I’m angry why they didn’t meet. If there is a complaint from the patient’s family, he is asked to complain to the Civil Surgeon.  

Dr. Comilla Civil Surgeon in this regard. Nachima Akhter said that the issue of Holy Care Hospital will be investigated.

Dhaka: At present, about one crore 31 lakh people are suffering from diabetes in the country. Bangladesh ranks eighth in the world in the number of patients suffering from diabetes.

Bangladesh Endocrine Society gave these information in a press conference organized on Monday (November 13) morning on ‘Know your diabetes risk and what to do’. Bangladesh Endocrine Society organized this press conference at Jahur Hossain Hall of National Press Club on the occasion of World Diabetes Day.

In the press conference, General Secretary of Bangladesh Endocrine Society and Bangabandhu Sheikh Mujib Medical University Associate Professor Dr. Shahzada Salim said, November 14 is World Diabetes Day. At present, about 1 crore 31 lakh people are suffering from diabetes in the country and Bangladesh ranks eighth in the world. Experts fear that this number may double in the next four years. As such, it is feared that the number of diabetes patients, one of the four non-communicable diseases of the country, may reach one and a half crore by 2025.

The press conference was also attended by the chief sponsor of Bangladesh Endocrine Society and director (education) of Bardem Hospital. Md. Farooq Pathan, President (elect) Professor Mohammad Hafizur Rahman, Vice President and Bardem Hospital Associate Professor Dr. Faria Afsana, member of the executive committee and expert doctor of Evercare Hospital. Ahsanul Haque Amin, Office Secretary and Assistant Professor of Bangabandhu Sheikh Mujib Medical University. Marufa Mostari.

These eminent doctors of the country said that Bangladesh is currently a diabetes-prone country in the world and the actual situation is more serious than that. At present, diabetes patients are increasing almost equally in urban and rural areas. Therefore taking into consideration the serious situation and taking effective measures to prevent it now is the need of the hour.

They also said that if action is not taken now, the situation will go out of control. It is very important to ensure coordinated action planning at all levels and from affordable drug delivery to other collaborative management in diabetic patient care.

About what to do to control diabetes, they said, 61.8 percent of the country’s patients do not know that they are suffering from diabetes. Because most patients don’t get regular diabetes checkups. So it is important for everyone, whether they have diabetes or not, to get tested for diabetes at least once a year. Along with reducing the amount of sugar in the food should be increased meat and vegetables. In addition, it is necessary to eat healthy food, avoid harmful food including fast food, walk regularly, play sports. If one is suffering from diabetes, then one should take the treatment as advised by the doctor.

These doctors said that it is necessary to take various measures at the national level to control diabetes.

Dhaka: Eight more people have died due to dengue infection in the last 24 hours. On this day, 1 thousand 740 people were admitted to hospital due to dengue.

This information was informed in a press release about dengue sent from the Health Emergency Operation Center and Control Room of the Department of Health on Monday (November 13).

It is said that in the last 24 hours, 1,740 people have been infected with dengue and have been admitted to the hospital. Among them, 330 people in Dhaka and 1,410 people in the whole country (except Dhaka City) were infected with dengue and admitted to hospital.

In the last 24 hours, a total of 1,898 dengue patients have been discharged from hospitals across the country. Among them, 371 people from different hospitals in Dhaka and 1 thousand 527 people from different hospitals all over the country (except in Dhaka) got clearance.

The notification also informed that eight people have died due to dengue in the last 24 hours. Of these, two died in Dhaka and six across the country (excluding Dhaka City).

A total of 1,484 people have died due to dengue this year. Among them, 865 people died in Dhaka and 619 people died in the whole country (excluding Dhaka City).

As of November 13 this year, a total of 293,572 people have been infected with dengue. Among them, 1 lakh 3 thousand 908 people in Dhaka and 1 lakh 89 thousand 664 people in the whole country (except Dhaka City) have been infected with dengue.

A total of two lakh 86 thousand 71 dengue patients have been discharged from the hospital so far this year. Among them, 1 lakh 1 thousand 498 people from different hospitals in Dhaka and 1 lakh 84 thousand 573 people from different hospitals across the country (except Dhaka City) have received clearance.

At present, a total of 6 thousand 17 dengue patients are admitted in hospitals across the country. 1 thousand 545 people in Dhaka and 4 thousand 472 patients in whole country (except Dhaka city) are admitted in hospital.

It is also said that in the current year, the rate of getting clearance from the hospital due to dengue is 97 percent. The hospitalization rate is 2 percent and the mortality rate is 0.5 percent.

From January 1 to December 31 last year, a total of 62 thousand 382 patients were admitted to various hospitals in the country and a total of 281 people died due to dengue.
